A map is a "snapshot" of a moment in history:

This is the earliest of the Ordnance Survey maps available.

The piece of land that Henry Babbage was to purchase in 1872 is contained within the area marked "720".

Park Road, Upper Park Road and Freelands Road do not yet exist.

Clearly shown are the features:

Bromley town centre.
Market Square.
High Street.
London Road.
Bromley College in London Road.  (Founded in 1666, this is a Grade I listed building and the oldest in Bromley).
Widmore Road.
Plaistow Lane.
Beckenham Lane.
Glassmill Lane.
Bromley Parish Church.
Bromley Palace.  (Built on the site of a manor house dating from around the year 1100, this is a Grade II listed building.  It became part of Bromley Civic Centre in 1982).
