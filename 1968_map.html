A map is a "snapshot" of a moment in history:

This map was produced by the Borough of Bromley in 1968.  It shows the new Harleyford and Bishops Green estates with the ten plots of land superimposed upon it.  These plots are marked "1" to "10".  The colours were added by myself.  The map would explain the reasons for the irregular shape of the boundaries of the Harleyford estate.

The purpose of this map was to facilitate the imposition of charges upon the new owner Park Rutland Investments (London) Ltd.  I do not understand the reason for such charges.

Here follows some further information concerning the plots of land:  The numbers relate to corresponding numbers on the map:

3 (yellow):
The Harleyford manor had been located near to the number "3".  Otherwise this area provided access to other areas.

2 (orange), 9 (blue) and 10 (pink):
The Harleyford apartments are located in these areas.

4 (orange):
The Ordnance Survey maps show that a small building was located here since 1933 at least.  This building was marked "The Cottage" on the 1959 and 1966 maps.  The area is now occupied by four lockup garages associated with the Harleyford estate.

6 (pink) and 7 (green):
These areas appear to have been parts of the back gardens of two Victorian houses in Plaistow Lane.

8 (light blue/green):
In this area was a large Victorian house that had belonged to the Church of England since the mid-1930s.  There is an unconfirmed rumour that originally it was an almshouse, but later it became the vicarage for St. John's Church in Park Road.  The Bishops Green apartments are partly located here.

1 (green) and 5 (blue):
There were two Victorian houses in these areas.  The Bishops Green apartments are partly located here.
