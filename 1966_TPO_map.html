A map is a "snapshot" of a moment in history

This is a diagram from a Tree Preservation Order dated 1966.
It relates to the property identified as "11 Upper Park Road".  This became the Harleyford estate. (The plan of Dainton Close appears distorted due to the overlapping of two pages).

The diagram shows that Harleyford manor house has been demolished.  The Harleyford and Bishops Green apartments have not yet been constructed.

The Tree Preservation Order identifies a number of trees on the Harleyford estate.  I have coloured in RED those that still exist today (2015), which are as follows:

T1 Plane
T2 Larch
T4 Yew
T5 Hawthorn
T8 Plane
T9 Sycamore
T10 Plain
T11 Plane
T12 Sycamore
T13 Lime
T15 Robinia
T16 Robinia

The trees T8 to T16 were planted in a straight line, presumably to support the demarcation of the boundary between the Harleyford and Dainton Close estates.

The London Plane trees (T8, T10 and T11) have today grown to an enormous size.  They are first shown on the 1895 map.  This species was quite popular in Victorian times due to their resistance to soot and pollution.  I estimate their age as about 140 years.
