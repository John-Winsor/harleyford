A map is a "snapshot" of a moment in history

In GREEN is shown the land that had belonged to Henry Babbage when he sold it in 1906.

All the surrounding roads have now been built, including the alley linking Upper Park Road with Freelands Road.
On some maps the alley is marked with the abbreviation "F.P." which means "Free Passage" or right-of-way.

The houses on the east side of Freelands Road have now been constructed.

Dainton and Harleyford manor houses are shown.

The ex-coach house is shown, adjacent to Dainton house.

Elsternwick House is shown near to the northern tip of the property.

On the north side of Upper Park Road, starting from the junction with Plaistow lane, is shown a row of five houses.
The first of these was demolished in recent times and was replaced with an apartment block.
The second is a fine old Victorian house, still standing (in 2015).
The next three houses no longer exist, and mark the location of Bishops Green.
The last of these became owned by the Church of England.
