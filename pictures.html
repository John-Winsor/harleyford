The superb picture of the Dainton and Harleyford manor houses was painted by Henry Babbage's brother Herschel.  Henry Babbage and his family lived in Dainton.  It was named after a house in Devon owned by his father, Charles Babbage.

Adjacent to Dainton is shown a group of small buildings which would include the coach house and stable, later to become converted into a residence.

Benjamin Herschel Babbage (1815 - 1878) was the eldest child of Charles and Georgiana Babbage.  Herschel's life was even more successful than that of Henry.  He has an extensive entry in the on-line encyclopaedia Wikipedia (whereas Henry is mentioned only in association with his father's mechanical computer).

In 1851 Herschel sailed to South Australia where he lived for the remainder of his life.  He went there to conduct a geological survey on behalf of the colonial government.  It was our good fortune that he took his painting with him.  Ultimately the painting was donated to the State Library of South Australia in Adelaide where it remains today.  Its image has been transmitted back to England by email in the form of a high resolution graphic.



This photograph, taken during the 1950s from the front of the ex-coach house residence, shows Harleyford manor in the background.
